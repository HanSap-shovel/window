# window (窓)

> *In an age where averages are fading, so each person can build their own standard.*

> I was eleven.
> A voice barked *"Hands up — click!"* and I thought I was dead.
> Then my stepfather's voice cut through.
> **"Run!"**

This text begins where the cry of an eleven-year-old who fled North Korea in October 2002 meets the account of a man, now in his thirties, who has become the father of three children.

---

## What is this project?

A book — **"Same Word, Different Inside"** (*『같은 말, 다른 속』*) — together with, on top of that book's structure, a tool — **the window (窓, *chang*)** — that each reader can build for themselves. A shared construction.

- The book does not give answers. It gives **a structure for finding them.**
- Each reader builds **their own tool** on that structure.
- The founder plants roots and steps back. **There is no owner.**

---

## Why make this public?

This system **cannot be completed alone.**

Eight billion people on this planet use the same word — "happiness." Yet none of them pictures the same thing. One person cannot see those eight billion different insides.

Strangers who are different from one another resonating with the same thing, and shaping the material together — that is how this tool works.

This is the reason the system is designed not as "a structure built alone" but as **"a structure shaped together."**

> For now this system can only be read in three languages: Korean, French, English. This is not a limit of the principle but **a limit of the author**. The language threshold can only be lowered by community translation — see the "Languages" section below.

---

## What this project does not pursue

- **No commercial monetization.** Visibility is treated only as a byproduct of shared completion.
- **No short-term self-help shortcuts.** This system works at the scale of generations.
- **No prescription of the form "live this way."** Only structure.

## How to shape it with us

Only five rules.

1. **Direction is set by the author.** Contributions are: questions, ordering, translation, sharing your own *second stage*.
2. **Do not lie.** Honesty is the only rule.
3. **Do not erase earlier answers.** Even when they change, we keep them. The trajectory of change is who we are.
4. **Do not escape into abstraction.** Always come back down to the concrete.
5. **Do not finish in one go.** Update across a lifetime.

Details: [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).

Two issue labels to start:

- `tension` — point out a contradiction, a question, an empty seat in the system.
- `weapon` — share the *second stage* (your own tool) you have built on this structure.
