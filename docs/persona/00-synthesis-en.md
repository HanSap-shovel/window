# 00 — Synthesis

## What this is

A nine-chapter letter woven from two days of thinking (2026-04-17 to 04-18). The author's first synthesis of the whole system as one readable piece. Meant to be read slowly, one chapter at a time.

---

## The nine chapters — a map

### 1. Where I come from
The root scene. Age six, in North Korea. The author, caught stealing food, is dragged home. The father is silent, repairing the roof. The mother says *"Go quickly and say you are sorry."* Approaching in tears of fear and guilt — and then the father, without a word, wraps the child in his arms. Four things are learned in that single embrace: love, father, guilt, fear. The father vanishes two years later and is never seen again. This one embrace is the foundation stone of the entire system.

### 2. The sickness I see in the world
Three names for three sicknesses.
- **Averaging** — eight billion people use the word *"happiness,"* but no two of them picture the same thing. Yet no one questions this.
- **Dopamine** — in a nearly-full society, people chase brief pleasure several times a day instead of building depth.
- **The empty cart** — a Korean proverb: *"An empty cart rattles loudest"* (*빈 수레가 요란하다*). The contemporary information economy has forgotten this.

### 3. What I believe
One sentence holds the absolute standard:

> **"What you do returns to you. The content is yours; the structure is shared."**

Good acts return as good. Evil acts return as evil. But the absolute standard is not good or evil itself — it is **the structure of action and return**. The content (what counts as good or evil) is each person's to build. Only the structure is universal.

### 4. What I am building — the three stages
- **Stage 1 · The book** — gives a *structure* for finding answers, not the answers themselves.
- **Stage 2 · The weapon** — each reader builds their own tool on top of that structure.
- **Stage 3 · The use** — the reader applies the weapon to themselves, not to others.

The founder hands out the weapon and steps back. *"I am a founder, not an owner."*

### 5. The inner work — five trainings
1. **From knowing to doing** — Wang Yangming's *知行合一* (knowing and doing are one).
2. **From desire to fullness** — *manjok* (滿足): the state of being full, with feet at rest.
3. **From forward to sideways** — instead of looking ahead, look at who is beside you, right now.
4. **Doubt as engine** — ask *"why?"* every day. The comfortable is not the right.
5. **Release the hurry** — the payment comes late. That is the price of a true beginning.

### 6. The outer work — examination is defense
The outer world is loud: noise marketing, algorithmic manipulation, pretense. But changing it does not start outside. **Self-knowledge is the longest-lasting defense.** Those who do not know themselves are those whom the outside will rush in to fill. And: *silent bystanders in a time of public lies become co-conspirators.*

### 7. My confession
The author, writing this, watches their own drift appear:

> **"I have lived 1. I now live 30. But I feel the 30 is not enough. I know it, and yet changing it approaches impossibility."**

The author registers themselves as the tool's **first user**. If the author cannot use it, it will not be given to the children.

### 8. Two kinds of love
- **The father's arm** — the action of *approaching* (the silent embrace at age six).
- **The mother's bosom** — the action of *waiting* (thirty years to reach the sentence: *"I can find happiness for my mother, but only my mother can feel it as happiness"*).

They are not exclusive. They are complementary. A whole person learns both.

### 9. The payment comes late
Kim Gu did not see independence. Solzhenitsyn wrote for decades before the Soviet Union fell. Seneca was ordered to die by Nero. The author may not see this system work in their own lifetime — perhaps not in their children's either.

> **"Do not rush. It simply takes time."**

— Closing letter: *To my wife.*
