# 21 April 2026, 8:30 — Doubt, Once More

> Morning thoughts. While driving, anger came up. I followed it all the way down.
> The raw, unedited version is preserved at the end of this document.

---

## 1. The slow car — waiting, enduring the pain

The car in front is going too slowly. Anger rises.

What is this anger aimed at, really? The slow car? The person driving slowly?
I imagine — the driver must be a woman, or an old man.
So I let the anger out and overtake. *Vroom.* I glance over.
Neither a woman, nor an old man.
And at the next red light, we come to a stop side by side.

How foolish.

To hate, to get angry — that is truly easy.
To endure, to forgive — that is hard; it is the life of the sage, what the ancients have done.

Shall I make myself a fool?
Or, by overcoming a little pain, shall I make myself a sage?
It is my choice, in the instant. It is my own reflection.

**Doubt, once more. Repeat the "why".**

---

## 2. The root of anger — why do "fools" make me angry?

Ordinarily, seeing fools makes me angry.
Is it because I am smart? No, I don't think so.

There are simply rules to follow, and some people don't follow them.
It isn't very difficult, so why?

Is it so hard to drive at 70 on a 70 road?
Why does that one go so slowly, and this one so fast?
Because it is dangerous, because it is a nuisance, because someone ends up paying the price — so yes, I get angry.

When traffic is jammed, I let exactly one person merge in front of me. Just one.
Letting two or more through may be kindness to them, but it is a nuisance to those behind me.
And seeing others who don't do the same makes me angry again.

But — someone, watching me, could get angry too, couldn't they?

**The reference point is oneself.** So is this anger really justified?
If anger eats away at life, isn't this precisely what must not be done?

---

## 3. The transfer of anger — spilling onto the ones who have nothing to do with it

We feel we must release this anger.
And so, people who are utterly unrelated are very likely to receive it.

When anger rises at work but rank and atmosphere won't allow its expression,
do we not carry it home and pour it out onto the children, onto the wife?

In life, fights start over trivial things —
but the trivial thing is only the *trigger*; it is not the *reason* for the fight.
We dig up mouldy old grudges to feed the anger.

So instead — drop the pride from the start.
All the things set aside because "my wife will probably think this…" —
bring them out in the moment, while the anger still wears a small shape, and speak them.
Try it that way.

---

## 4. Doubting "team spirit" — the problem is in the system

These days I am preparing to change jobs; I have changed jobs before.
Something I have always noticed: what the company — the team — is looking for
is someone with "team spirit", a way of thinking that fits the team.

I have come to believe that this itself is a sign that **the team has a problem**.

A truly well-built team, with its culture and its system,
would grow whoever arrives — even a newcomer — into the shape of the team.
Why, then, specifically search for such a person?

This is, clearly, a problem of the system.

Of course, if what is being sought is a communal mindset — someone moving with others toward a shared goal — that is another matter.
But even then — if the team is truly well-designed,
it should not *need* someone who already has this;
it should be able to *make* it in anyone. Shouldn't it?

Doesn't your society simply need someone to do the work for it?
Is it not to lighten one's own load that such a person is needed?
Is that not what you have called "team spirit"?

The foundation of society is the family, and beyond that friends, the company, the school, the country.
If a problem is to be recognised and changed, it must begin **from the smallest unit**.

---

## 5. The contradiction of utopia — what I actually want

Pouring out thoughts like this, an idea surfaces.

*What am I really trying to do, writing all this?*

A utopia? A society in which everyone attains liberation,
a world where no one hurts or is hurt — is this what I am dreaming of as I write?

It is a contradiction, truly.

I get angry, I think, I seek liberation, I seek awakening —
and I have lived wishing that everyone would do the same.

Then — have I been liberated? Awakened? No.

Dozens of times a day I grow angry, I grow irritated,
and I spill onto people who have nothing to do with it — and it repeats, and repeats.

I try to reduce it; I search for the root of the anger.
This anger — *is it not an anger a sick society has injected into me at the root?*
To think the root, to pose the question, to search for another self: that is the process.

---

**It is not about making everyone reach that point.**

A world in which everyone is awakened, liberated, would have no reason to exist.
For there would be no reason to progress.
Nothing left to pursue — a world that could no longer exist, and so a world that does not exist.

For now, I simply, for each small thing, question and doubt and think once more —

**and what I dream of is not a world where no one hurts anyone,
but a world full of people who *try* to hurt others as little as possible.**

---

## Closing

Doubt, once more.
Repeat the "why".
